Toasted Bread Crumbs

by Chef Glenn Rolnick

This quick and inexspensive topping goes well on salads, meatloafs, and other dishes. This recipe appears in Carmine's Celebrates

Total Time

Under 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 slices white bread, crusts removed
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chopped garlic
  • 2 tablespoons grated Romano cheese
  • 3 teaspoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

Instructions

  1. Dry the bread on a cooling rack, uncovered, overnight. Pulse the dried bread in a food processor until chopped into fine crumbs.
  2. Preheat a large sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and garlic and sauté until the garlic is lightly browned.
  3. Add the bread crumbs, reduce the heat to medium, and sauté until the crumbs take on an even brown color; do not burn. Toss the bread crumbs frequently; the process should take about 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer the bread crumbs to a plate and let cool. When the crumbs are at room temperature, combine them in a bowl with the cheese and parsley and toss to mix. Store in a tightly covered container in a cool, dry place until ready to use. They will keep for about a week.

Enjoy!
